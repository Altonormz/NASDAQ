Consumer stocks were finishing moderately lower late in Friday trading, with the Consumer Staples Select Sector SPDR Fund (XLP) dropping 0.5% and the Consumer Discretionary Select Sector SPDR Fund (XLY) retreating 0.6%.
In company news, El Pollo Loco Holdings (LOCO) dropped 8.1% after the restaurant chain reported a decline in non-GAAP net income to $0.16 per share during its Q4 ended Dec. 28 compared with a $0.17 per share adjusted profit during the year-ago period.
Allbirds (BIRD) slumped 48% after its fiscal Q4 net loss almost doubled compared with year-ago levels as revenue fell, with the December quarter results trailing analyst estimates. The footwear company also said chief financial officer Mike Bufano would step down from the post on April 24.
Among gainers, Adecoagro (AGRO) was fractionally higher after the farm products company reported a decline in non-GAAP Q4 net income compared with year-ago levels, falling to $18.6 million from $57.1 million during the final three months of 2021 but still beating analyst estimates for the December quarter.
Barnes & Noble Education (BNED) added 7% after the specialty publisher reported an 11% year-over-year increase in its fiscal Q3 sales, rising to $447.1 million and beating the Capital IQ consensus for the three months ended Jan. 28 by $50 million.
