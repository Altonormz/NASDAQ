Consumer stocks were broadly mixed in Thursday trading, with the Consumer Staples Select Sector SPDR Fund (XLP) climbing 0.4% and the Consumer Discretionary Select Sector SPDR Fund (XLY) sinking 1.1%.
In company news, BJ's Wholesale Club Holdings (BJ) gained 3.9% after reporting non-GAAP Q4 net income of $1.00 per share on $4.93 billion in sales, beating the Capital IQ consensus call looking for an $0.89 per share adjusted profit on $4.79 billion in sales for the three months ended Jan. 28.
Sovos Brands (SOVO) rose 7.9% after the packaged foods company topped analyst estimates with its fiscal Q4 earnings and sales and also projected between $900 million to $925 million in 2023 sales, also exceeding the $887.9 million Street view.
Helen of Troy (HELE) fell 8.1% after the cosmetics company said Chief Financial Officer Matt Osberg will step down on April 27 to pursue another opportunity and will be succeeded on an interim basis by retired CFO Brian Grass.
